<p><p>The glucose market primarily consists of two types: D-Glucose Monohydrate and Anhydrous Glucose. D-Glucose Monohydrate is a crystalline form that contains one molecule of water, offering stability and easy handling, making it suitable for pharmaceuticals, food, and beverage applications. Anhydrous Glucose, on the other hand, is a dehydrated form without water, leading to higher concentration and lower weight, preferred in food processing and energy products. Both types play vital roles in various industries, catering to specific application needs.</p></p>
