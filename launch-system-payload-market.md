<p><p>The launch system payload market is categorized based on orbital altitudes: Geosynchronous Earth Orbit (GEO), Medium Earth Orbit (MEO), and Low Earth Orbit (LEO). GEO is primarily used for telecommunications and weather satellites, providing stable long-term positioning. MEO serves navigation satellites and reconnaissance, balancing coverage and latency. LEO, featuring lower altitudes, is ideal for satellite constellations, Earth observation, and emerging space technologies, offering reduced latency and launch costs, driving commercial interest and innovation in the space sector.</p></p>
