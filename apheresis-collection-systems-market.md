<p><p>Apheresis collection systems are vital in separating blood components for therapeutic and donation purposes. The centrifugation method uses centrifugal force to separate blood into its components based on density, enabling targeted collection of specific cells like platelets or plasma. In contrast, membrane filtration employs specialized filters that allow certain blood constituents to pass through while retaining others, facilitating separation based on size and permeability. Both technologies cater to diverse medical needs, enhancing patient care and blood product availability.</p></p>
