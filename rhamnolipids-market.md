<p><p>Rhamnolipids are biosurfactants produced by microorganisms, primarily Pseudomonas aeruginosa, known for their emulsifying, foaming, and antimicrobial properties. They are categorized into types R1, R2, R3, and R4 based on the number of rhamnose units and fatty acid chains. R1 has one rhamnose unit, R2 has two, while R3 and R4 are more complex with additional fatty acid chains. These variants are used in industries like agriculture, cosmetics, pharmaceuticals, and bioremediation, with R2 being the most commercially prevalent due to its balanced properties and cost-effectiveness.</p></p>
