<p><strong>The Food Allergy Immunotherapies Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Subcutaneous Immunotherapy</li><li>Sublingual Immunotherapy</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Food allergy immunotherapies primarily include two types: subcutaneous immunotherapy (SCIT) and sublingual immunotherapy (SLIT). SCIT involves the administration of allergen extracts via injection, gradually increasing doses to build tolerance. It's typically administered in clinical settings, requiring regular visits. In contrast, SLIT introduces allergens through dissolvable tablets or drops under the tongue, allowing for self-administration at home, which enhances convenience and compliance. Both therapies aim to reduce allergic reactions and improve quality of life for individuals with food allergies.</p></p>
