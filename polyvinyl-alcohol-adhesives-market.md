<p><p>Polyvinyl alcohol adhesives are classified into low viscosity and high viscosity types based on their fluidity and application suitability. Low viscosity adhesives are ideal for thin films and precise applications, offering good penetration into porous substrates, making them suitable for paper, textiles, and lightweight materials. In contrast, high viscosity adhesives provide stronger bonding and are used in applications requiring thicker films or higher adhesive strength, such as in woodworking and construction, where durability and load-bearing capacity are essential.</p></p>
